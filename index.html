Claude Monet was a French Impressionist painter who captured the beauty of nature through his unique style of emphasizing light and color

Born in 1840 in Paris, Monet spent much of his life in Normandy, where he was captivated by the beauty of the natural world.

He was particularly drawn to the play of light and color on water, which he captured in his famous series of water lily paintings.

Despite facing criticism, he persisted and became one of the greatest painters of all time.
